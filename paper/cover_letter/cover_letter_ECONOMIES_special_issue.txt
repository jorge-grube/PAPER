Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript "From Regime Detection to Decision Rules: A Data-Driven Macro-Financial CVaR Framework for European Multi-Asset Portfolios" for consideration in the Special Issue "Next-Generation Macroeconomics: Data-Driven and Artificial Intelligence Approaches" in Economies.

The paper fits the scope of the Special Issue directly. It develops and evaluates a transparent data-driven regime framework that applies a four-state Gaussian Hidden Markov Model (HMM), estimated on eight weekly macro-financial indicators, to Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) portfolio optimization across European multi-asset portfolios over 2000 to 2026. The paper addresses the Special Issue's core themes at multiple levels:

- Data-driven macro-financial regime detection using high-frequency macro and market data (VIX, VSTOXX, sovereign spreads, HICP inflation, yield-curve slope, ESI);
- Interest-rate and sovereign-risk episodes specific to the European context (2011 sovereign crisis, 2022 ECB rate-hiking cycle);
- Implementation frictions that limit the practical value of AI/machine-learning state detection systems;
- Transparent, interpretable decision rules that bridge regime detection and portfolio implementation.

The central finding, that HMM regime detection succeeds but naive regime-conditional CVaR allocation fails through excessive turnover, while implementation-aware alternatives substantially recover the performance gap, offers a concrete and policy-relevant lesson for next-generation macro-financial system design.

The manuscript is original, has not been previously published, and is not currently under consideration at any other journal. Empirical results are fully out-of-sample, based on a walk-forward expanding-window design. Raw data are proprietary LSEG/Refinitiv; processed data and Python code sufficient to reproduce all tables and figures are available upon request subject to licensing restrictions.
