PLANNED PAPER ABSTRACT — MDPI Economies Special Issue Submission
Special Issue: "Next-Generation Macroeconomics: Data-Driven and Artificial Intelligence Approaches"

Title: From Regime Detection to Decision Rules: A Data-Driven Macro-Financial CVaR Framework for European Multi-Asset Portfolios

Abstract (approx. 250 words):

The growing availability of high-frequency macro-financial data and advances in machine-learning methods have made it feasible to identify latent economic states in near-real time. This paper asks whether such data-driven regime detection can be translated into improved out-of-sample portfolio allocation, or whether implementation frictions prevent detected states from generating practical value.

We develop and evaluate a transparent macro-financial regime framework that combines a four-state Gaussian Hidden Markov Model (HMM), estimated on eight weekly macro-financial indicators covering volatility, sovereign spreads, yield-curve slope, inflation pressure, and economic sentiment, with Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) portfolio optimization across European multi-asset portfolios spanning January 2000 to April 2026. Using a strictly out-of-sample walk-forward design with no forward-looking information, we evaluate naive regime-conditioned CVaR allocation, two implementation-aware alternatives (turnover-penalized CVaR and regime-constrained weight bands), and a sovereign fixed-income expansion adding German, Spanish, and Italian government bond indices.

Our central finding is that HMM regime detection is reliable, achieving greater than 94 percent adjacent-window label agreement, but that naive regime-conditional CVaR allocation is dominated by static (regime-unconditional) CVaR due to structural implementation friction. Static CVaR achieves a Sharpe ratio of 0.530 over the 2010 to 2026 evaluation window. Naive regime filtering raises annual portfolio turnover to 226 percent, collapsing net performance. Implementation-aware alternatives recover much of the cost gap (net Sharpe 0.486 to 0.519). Sovereign bond inclusion materially reduces drawdowns but introduces duration risk that materializes in the 2022 European Central Bank rate-hiking cycle.

These results offer a practical lesson for next-generation data-driven macroeconomic systems: reliable regime detection is a necessary but not sufficient condition for improved portfolio outcomes. The bottleneck is the design of transparent, stable, and cost-aware decision rules. Regime-constrained weight bands, encoding investment policy beliefs as interpretable guardrails, provide a viable bridge between macro-state detection and portfolio implementation. The findings are directly relevant to systemic risk monitoring, interest-rate risk management, and the design of AI-assisted allocation frameworks in European multi-asset markets.
